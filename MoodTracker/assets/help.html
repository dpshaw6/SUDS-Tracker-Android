‘Rate’ Your Mood

 

The Rate tab allows you to rate your mood. T2 Mood Tracker comes pre-programmed with six mood categories: Anxiety, Depression, General Well-Being, Head Injury, Post-Traumatic Stress, and Stress. You can create custom mood categories by tapping + Add Rating Category in the menu for the Rate screen.

To rate a mood category, tap the category name. This takes you to a screen populated with sliders. The left end of the slider shows a negative feeling, and the other end shows a positive feeling. Simply move the slider toward the end that best reflects how you currently feel. Stronger feelings should be rated more to the extreme edges, while milder feelings will trend closer to the middle.

When you are done with your ratings, touch save to return to the Rate tab. Your rating is automatically included on the graph for that mood category.

On the Rate tab (and any other tab), you can also add a note by touching Add Note from the menu button.

Reviewing Your ‘Results’

 

The Results tab allows you to view all of your ratings by date, manage reports, and review your previous notes.

Graph Results

Selecting Graph Results takes you to a graph showing your previous ratings. When in portrait view, the graph provides multiple configuration options. When in landscape view, the graph takes up the entire screen to make viewing results easier. Each point on the graph represents the average of your ratings for that mood category for that specific date and time. Data points toward the top of the graph represent higher levels of each mood category (high Anxiety, high General Well-Being) while ratings toward the bottom of the graph represent lower levels.

Use pinch and zoom to narrow and increase your data views, and simply swipe the graph left or right to pan through the data points.

Customizing Your Results

Sharing results is easy! Touch Save Screenshot in the menu to save a screenshot of your current graph. Using this function allows you to quickly share that screenshot via the different share options you currently have on your device (e.g. email, messaging, etc.). All screenshots are saved in the T2MoodTracker folder on your SD card.

Customizing the color & symbols used in the graph – Tapping on the symbol to the left of a category or scale takes you the Customize Chart Icon view. Select a color and the desired symbol then hit OK. You can also customize how the individual feelings within a mood category appear by selecting the name of the category while in the Graph Results screen, and follow the same process.

When in portrait view, the Categories, Notes, and Options buttons allow you to configure the graph how you want it to look.

Categories – This button allows you to control which categories you see on the graph. Flip each category’s switch On or Off to update the graph appropriately. Tap a category name to see the individual negative/positive scales for that category. This allows you to view the graph for the individual feeling scales within one mood category. Like the category view, flipping a switch On or Off will update the graph appropriately. Tapping on either Categories or Scales will alternately hide the information pane, and then show it again. To return to the category view, use the back function.

Notes – This button shows your saved notes starting with the most recent. Tap a specific note to see a full-screen view of the note.

Options – This button allows you to configure many options on the graph. Like the other views, using the On/Off switches will turn those options on and off. 

·         Data Range allows you to select the default data range the graph results show. Use the selector to choose 30 days, 90 days, 180 days, or 1 Year. This restricts the data shown on the graph to the range selected. These options should be experimented with if you have a large amount of data points and want to speed up the chart generation process. You can access data beyond 1 year by exporting the data to a CSV or PDF report.

·         Toggle Symbols: add/remove random symbols to the graph lines to help better distinguish the category data. To customize the symbol used for a category or scale, tap on the symbol to the left of the category or scale name. This opens the Customize Chart Icon screen. From here, select a color and use the arrows to scroll through available symbols. Once satisfied with your selections, confirm by touching OK.

·         Toggle Lines: add/remove a colored line to the category data.

·         Toggle Shading: add/remove a shaded gradient to the category data.

View Notes

This button shows your saved notes starting with the most recent. Tap a specific note to see a full-screen view of the note. From here, you can update the note with additional information or delete a note. Be careful with this option, as all deletions are permanent. You can add a new note from any screen by clicking Add Note in the menu.

Saved Reports

This button takes you to the Saved Reports page where you can view your saved reports. Tapping on a report provides you with the option to either Email the report, or to View it (note: to view a PDF file, or a CSV file, you’ll need the appropriate application loaded on your device).

Pressing a report name for a long hold gives you the option to delete that specific report. Be careful with this option, as all deletions are permanent unless you have that report saved elsewhere.

Create Reports

Selecting Create Reports brings up a report generator. From here, you can specify exactly what data to include in a report.

·         Date Range will define the start and end point for the data you include. Tap either From Date or To Date and use the date selector.

·         Format of the report can be controlled by selecting either CSV or PDF formats for the output of the report.

·         Categories allows you to select which categories to include in the report. Use the On/Off checkbox to control that output.

·         Notes determines whether or not to include your notes with the report. Use the On/Off checkbox to control that output.

When you have all the parameters set, select the Export button at the bottom.

·         Saving as a CSV exports the data range selected to a CSV formatted file and saves it on your SD card in the T2 Mood Tracker folder. You can share this file from the Saved Reports screen, where you can select and review your saved reports.

·         Saving as a PDF exports the data range selected to a PDF formatted file and saves it on your SD card in the T2MoodTracker folder. You can share this file from the Saved Reports screen, where you can select and review your saved reports. PDF reports are easy to read and include a chart of each category, as well as the scale information for each category. Linear regression trend lines are included to help determine the overall trend of the data.

‘Support’ Functions

 

The Support tab provides some communication and resource-location options.

About T2 Mood Tracker provides background information on why T2 Mood Tracker was created and the National Center for Telehealth & Technology.

Feedback opens an email with our email address. Send us your feedback so we can continue improving T2 Mood Tracker.

Help links you to this help file (so we guess you already found it!). Help can also be accessed at any time from the menu

Rate App links to the marketplace where you downloaded T2 Mood Tracker. Rate the app and provide some feedback for other potential users.

Tell a Friend sends a link to the marketplace where you downloaded T2 Mood Tracker to a friend.

Local Resources/Help provides information about the Defense Centers of Excellence (DCoE) Outreach Center, including the email address and toll free number to locate local resources for psychological health information or assistance.

Backup Database saves a backup of your data in the T2MoodTracker folder on your SD card. Make sure you use this routinely to prevent a loss of your data through phone changes, or uninstalls of the app. You can transfer this file from your SD card to other locations to prevent loss if your phone is lost to you.

Restore Database allows you to restore saved data from the Backup Database function. If you use this function, it will overwrite any existing information, so use it cautiously.

‘Settings’

 

The Settings tab takes you to the heart of customizing T2 Mood Tracker. Within Settings, you can add and edit rating categories, setup reminders, turn unused category notifications, startup tips, and anonymous data transmission on or off, enable security, and clear old data.

Add/Edit Rating Categories

This button takes you to the Add/Edit Rating Categories screen. This area contains the six pre-programmed categories included in T2 Mood Tracker and any custom categories you added. Touch a category to delete it or edit its name, scales, etc. The six pre-programmed categories cannot be deleted or modified, but you can choose whether or not they display in your Rate tab area by toggling the Show on main screen? checkmark for each category.

To add a new category, touch the Add Rating Category button in the menu. This will open up the category editing screen, and immediately prompt you for the category name. Once you’ve selected a name, you’ll need to decide if your new category is a desirable trait. If you accidentally indicate that a negative trait, such as sadness or anger, is desirable, it will display incorrectly in your graphed results.

 

Before you can use this new category, you’ll need to add in scales. You can do this via the Add Rating Scale button in the menu. You’ll be queried to use an existing scale (one already built by you or used in the pre-programmed categories), or to create a new one.

 

If selecting from an existing scale, simply scroll down until you find the one you want to use and tap on it. If creating a new scale, fill in what the undesirable and desirable traits are, e.g., Sad/Happy, then choose the save button. Continue this process until satisfied with the scales for your new category. You can add up to 10 different scales for a category.

 

Reminder

You can set up to three different reminders a day so you don't forget to make your ratings.

Notify Unused Categories

Turning this function on will remind you if you have categories that you don’t regularly use.

Show Tips on Startup

This allows you to turn the health tips on the initial splash screen On or Off. You can also disable the startup tips on the initial splash screen.

Send Anonymous Data

Knowing how T2 Mood Tracker is being used helps the developers modify and upgrade the app based on anonymous usage patterns. This information DOES NOT compromise your personal anonymity in any way. You may disable this function by selecting Off.

Security

This area allows you to choose a PIN to secure T2 Mood Tracker on your device and protect your data. When you select your PIN, you establish two security questions so you can recover your PIN if you forget it. You can deactivate this feature by inputting your current PIN and then clearing the function.

Clear Data

This allows you to clear all ratings and/or notes from a category. Tap on a category or the notes section to clear the data. You will receive a confirmation message to complete the deletion. If you choose Ok, all the data from that category will be deleted. Be careful with this option, as all deletions are permanent.
